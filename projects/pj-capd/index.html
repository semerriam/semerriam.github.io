The Center for American Politics and Design (CAPD) is a research group founded in 2018 that maintains an archive of every logo for candidates running in Congressional, Presidential and Gubernatorial races in the United States.

This project is done in collaboration with Lukas Bentel, Will Denton, Seth Kranzler, and Kevin Wiesner.

We maintain social media profiles for the group to share recent news or inspiraton on the topic of design in politics:

CAPD Website

A tool for exploring the archive by design and political characteristics


Designed in collaboration with other CAPD co-founders, the branding and website design are inspired by the U.S. Bicentennial design by Bruce Blackburn and iconic symbols of Americana including the firecracker popsicle.

The archive initially was built only weeks before the 2018 Congressional elections by extracting logos from each candidates' campaign websites and social profiles and manually entering in design atrributes. We used FiveThirtyEight and Cook Political Report designations for political attributes such as competitiveness of district and incumbency data.

Over the past several years, we have worked with a number of news outlets such as The Washington Post and The Pudding on stories around design and politics. We sometimes will critique logo designs, provide historical context, or be invited to speak on the subject.

Additionally, each campaign season, we share some simple summary charts with outlets indicating larger trends in the dataset such as color or name emphasis of the logos.
